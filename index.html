Specialized Therapy Services in Santa Monica

I offer evidence-based, trauma-informed treatment tailored to your unique needs and goals.

Anxiety & Panic Therapy

I work with clients who experience persistent worry, panic attacks, difficulty sleeping, or a sense that they're always bracing for something to go wrong. My approach helps you understand both the emotional and physiological sides of what you're experiencing.

  • Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) techniques
  • Managing constant worry and tension
  • Breaking the cycle of overthinking
  • Developing sustainable coping strategies

Trauma & EMDR Therapy

Trauma work is an important part of my practice. Whether you've experienced single-incident trauma or more complex, long-standing patterns, I use trauma-informed techniques including EMDR to help clients feel more regulated and safe in their daily lives.

  • EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)
  • Single-incident and complex trauma treatment
  • Stabilization and grounding techniques
  • Building resilience and sense of safety

Burnout & Professional Stress

I frequently support clients dealing with professional burnout, perfectionism, and high internal pressure. Many are entrepreneurs, creatives, or professionals who feel disconnected from themselves after years of pushing through stress.

  • Recognizing and addressing burnout patterns
  • Managing perfectionism and high standards
  • Work-life balance and boundary setting
  • Sustainable approaches to productivity

My Approach to Therapy

I take a warm, collaborative, and grounded approach to therapy. Sessions are structured enough to be supportive, while still leaving space for reflection and depth. I integrate evidence-based methods to help clients understand both the emotional and physiological sides of what they're experiencing.

My therapeutic approach includes:

  • Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) for practical skill-building
  • EMDR for trauma processing and integration
  • Trauma-informed techniques focused on safety and stabilization
  • Mind-body awareness and regulation strategies

I believe therapy works best when clients feel respected, understood, and actively involved in the process. My goal is not just symptom relief, but helping clients develop insight, resilience, and a stronger relationship with themselves over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you accept insurance for therapy services?

+

I am an out-of-network provider, which means I do not bill insurance directly. However, many clients are able to receive partial reimbursement from their insurance company for out-of-network mental health services. I can provide you with a superbill (detailed receipt) that you can submit to your insurance for potential reimbursement. I recommend contacting your insurance provider to understand your out-of-network benefits before beginning therapy.

What are your session rates and payment options?

+

My standard session rate is $250 for a 50-minute individual therapy session. Payment is due at the time of service, and I accept credit cards, debit cards, and HSA/FSA cards. I also offer a limited number of sliding scale spots for clients experiencing financial hardship. Please inquire during our initial consultation to discuss your specific situation.

Do you currently have openings for new clients?

+

My availability varies throughout the year. I currently have limited openings for new clients on Tuesday and Thursday afternoons. I also offer some early morning slots for telehealth sessions. If my schedule is full, I maintain a waitlist and will reach out when space becomes available. I also provide referrals to other qualified therapists in the Santa Monica area who may have more immediate availability.

Do you offer telehealth or in-person sessions?

+

Yes, I offer both in-person therapy sessions at my Santa Monica office and secure telehealth sessions for clients anywhere in California. Many clients choose to alternate between in-person and telehealth depending on their schedule and preferences. In-person sessions are held at my private office located at 123th Street 45 W, Santa Monica, CA 90401, which is easily accessible with nearby parking.

What can I expect in the first therapy session?

+

The first session is an opportunity for us to get to know each other and determine if we're a good fit. I'll ask about what brought you to therapy, your current concerns, relevant background information, and what you're hoping to achieve. This is also your chance to ask questions about my approach, experience, and how we might work together. By the end of our first meeting, we'll discuss whether continuing therapy together makes sense and create an initial treatment plan tailored to your needs.

How long does therapy typically take?

+

The length of therapy varies depending on your individual needs and goals. Some clients find relief and achieve their goals within a few months of weekly sessions, while others benefit from longer-term work. I regularly check in about your progress and adjust our approach as needed. My goal is to help you develop the insight and skills you need to thrive independently, not to keep you in therapy indefinitely.
